A Sudden Change of Plans

Posted on 08 October 2024 12:13 NZDT by Michael Keith

Category: News

At the end of August - almost 29 years to the day since I begun my career in the games industry - I resigned and entered into what I’ve been describing as “retirement”. The quotes indicating that I haven’t quite decided what form it will take.

Quitting had been on my mind for a while, in that typically vague wishy washy “oh, I dunno - sometime in the future” way, but a surprise decision by company execs to pursue a business partnership I felt was deeply unethical and problematic suddenly brought the need to make a change front and centre with a real jolt. C’est la vie - I have zero regrets and feel so much better for it.

While I’m unsure I have the energy to return to a games industry utterly unrecognisable from the one I entered in 1995, I find myself more fascinated than ever by how we experience and engage with the world around us, and the role technology can play in encouraging and facilitating this. 12+ years of work and research in Augmented Reality, Spatial Computing and geographic/location based experiences have shown me that the Black Mirror-esque “AI” powered AR glasses future pushed by the likes of meta is not the way we should be heading. There is a different future with tech respecting the user and environment while effectively getting out of the way that - in my opinion - is far more interesting and exciting.

Over time I’ve cobbled together a document I aspirationally refer to as my manifesto (if only it were coherent enough to qualify as one) which brings together a whole range of fields I feel are interesting and connected. As I continue to ruminate on and explore this space, I hope to post occasional rambles about it all.

Or I may just sit on the couch and re-watch “Columbo” episodes…
