This month in... The One '94

Posted on 25 August 2020 20:07 NZST by Michael Keith

Category: This month in...

theone199408

News

While Commodore was still without a buyer, word leaked that the company leading the pack was in fact Samsung who were looking to gain more of a foothold in the European market. It seemed everything was hanging on that deal.

Dominik Diamond was rumoured to be returning to “Gamesmaster”.

A report from Mintel showed that the UK games industry contracted by 4% last year. The market value in 1993 was Â£670 million compared to Â£700 million in 1992. One theory was falling software prices.

Music giant BMG announced they were making a move into games.

Team 17 had a busy few months ahead with planned releases of “Super Stardust”, “Overdrive”, “Alien Breed 2: Tower Assault”, “All Terrain Racing” and “King of Thieves”. The latter was particularly interesting as it was being developed by Rico Holmes and Andreas Tadic of “Alien Breed” fame, though it never saw a release.

Commodore’s schedule for the months leading up to Christmas showed they hoped to have 423 titles out for the CD32, almost double from what it was at this point (197). While that sounds impressive, it was largely made up of CD-i video discs and old CDTV “gardening hits and karaoke”.

Blockbuster coughed up Â£135 million to buy out Virgin Interactive Entertainment.

The biggest consumer entertainment show in the UK, Live ‘94, was to take place at Earls Court from September 20th to 25th. Around 200,000 visitors were expected.

Game industry body ELPSA launched three raids on pirates to recover 500 CDs of software in a haul apparently valued at Â£10 million.

It hadn’t been long since the release of “Frontier”, but “The First Encounter” - standalone followup - was due for release in October and promised a number of significant improvements (particularly “less bugs”). While it was released for PC, it wouldn’t end up gracing the Amiga.

Distributor ZCL announced a series of peripherals. There was a CD drive for the A1200 costing Â£199.99 and an adapter for the CD32 allowing the user to connect a keyboard and floppy drive. They also had “two new types of storage card” delivering between 20mb and 340mb capacity.

Despite losing deals with LucasArts, SSI and Novalogic, US Gold were bullish about their prospects as they opened a new development office in Oxford called Silicon Dreams. Apparently LucasArts games had only made up 3% of their total sales.

Software house Zeppelin was snapped up by US publisher Merit Software for a Â£2.2 million.

After months of speculation, 20th Century Fox opened a games division which would release its first title “Pagemaster” in November (which would be developed by Probe). Seems it only made it out on PC and handhelds.

UK high street retailer Currys was set to close 80 town centre stores with 20 more likely to follow as the economic downtown took hold. Boots had also reported game sales had dropped by a third during the first quarter of the year.

Sales chart company Gallup were to increase the number of retailers contributing to the numbers to 3250, up from 2000.

Microvalue’s “Soccer Superstars” would feature the logo of sports company Mitre in a licensing deal.

Features

On this month’s two cover disks:

  • “Kid Chaos” Ocean (demo)
  • “Drelbs” Simon Chin (PD)
  • “Wild Cup Soccer” Millennium (demo)
  • “Vital Light” Millennium (demo)

An interview with Paul Carrington and Lisa Tunnah who had formed Vulcan Software and recently released their first game, “Valhalla”, after a mere 5 months of development time.

The One called out software publishers and developers for talking guff about how great their upcoming games were going to be (which then turned out to be naff), in an article titled “Liars!”.

Andrew Braybrook highlighted some games which he reckoned had pushed the Amiga’s technical capabilities. They were:

  • “Elfmania” Renegade
  • “Stardust” Daze Marketing
  • “Galactic” Satvros Fasoulas
  • “Turrican 1 & 2” Factor 5/Rainbow Arts
  • “Agony” Psygnosis

Getting the in-depth Work in Progress treatment this month:

  • “Nova Storm” Psygnosis (unreleased)
  • “Simon the Sorcerer 2” Adventuresoft
  • “King Pin” Team 17

The One checked in on where “Rise of the Robots” and “Zeewolf” - two games “stuck in development hell” - were at.

The final instalment of the “Sensible World of Soccer” dev diary and Jon Hare stopped by the mag for a chat.

Public Domain games reviewed:

  • “Drelbs” 84%
  • “Glaxo” 79%
  • “Obliteratives” 82%
  • “Mucus” 52%
  • “Better Dead than Alien” 81%

Speaking of Public Domain games, there was a feature on PD coin op clones which included:

  • “Bob’s Garden” (“Mr Do”)
  • “Donkey Kong” (er, no guesses)
  • “Digger” (“Dig Dug”)
  • “Hemroids” (“Asteroids”)
  • “Gorf” (“Gorf”)
  • “Mouthman” (“Pacman”)
  • “Croak” (“Frogger”)

Notable Previews

  • “Dawn Patrol” Empire
  • “Planet Football” Infogrames (unreleased?)
  • “Fields of Glory” Microprose
  • “Operation G2” Psygnosis (unreleased)

Notable Reviews

  • “Ruff ‘N’ Tumble” Wunderkind/Renegade - 91% “Everything about it hangs together brilliantly; the graphic style is perfect, the gameplay finely-tuned, and the pumping soundtrack requires two huge speakers and sympathetic neighbours to show it at its best. Yes, I know it’s a bit embarrassing, and I’m sure you’re aware of how much I like to moan, but Ruff ’n’ Tumble really is the apex of platform pleasure.”
  • “Wild Cup Soccer” Teque/Millennium - 81% “I enjoyed Wild Cup Soccer - in two-player mode, anyway. Charging around, mashing the opposition in a variety of satisfyingly gory and violent ways, is a right hoot and no mistaking.”
  • “Vital Light” Efecto Caos/Millennium - 81% “… the early levels are tediously easy and the whole thing smacks of a poor man’s Tetris. Dig a bit deeper, though, and you discover that the game - despite its aesthetics - is a shoot-’em-up at heart, but one which requires as much forward planning as Tetris, Klax and their ilk.”
  • “Putty Squad” System 3 - 90% “All of the things that let Putty down first time round have been improved a thousand times over, with major gameplay improvements, excellent level layouts, and some really good toys to get your teeth into.” (this didn’t see a release until 2013)
  • “Kid Chaos” Magnetic Fields/Ocean - 87% “What we have is a lightning-fast platformer, running easily as quickly as Sonic on the Mega Drive, and there’s even an impressive number of enemies in there too. Add to that the most layers of parallax scrolling that we’ve seen since Lionheart, a thumping soundtrack and a range of spot-on sound effect, and you’re looking at a very impressive game.”
