This month in... The One '94

30 November 2020·4 mins
This Month In... TheOne Magazines Amiga 1994 November

News #

Editor Simon Byron announced he was stepping down to work at a chip shop on the outskirts of Bognor: “I’ve decided that the life of a simple chippy is preferable to slogging my guts out on Britain’s least popular Amiga games magazine.” Andy Nuttall would be filling in as Acting Editor.

Frustrating times for Amiga journos and fans alike as there was still no news from Commodore on the future of the company.

I’m not sure if this was an indication on the dearth of news, but Impression’s had hired Daze Marketing to represent their upcoming titles: “Breach 3” (unreleased), “High Seas Trader”, “Lords of the Realm” and “Front Lines” (unreleased).

“Manga Mania” - apparently “the only British title to feature top Japanese Manga comic strips” - was getting a reboot/relaunch.

French conglomerate Chargeurs acquired a 24% stake of Ocean. The Manchester software house were looking to expand their operations in Europe and America, with new titles costing $250,000 to $2 million to develop.

Time Warner Interactive hoped that “Primal Rage” would be the next “Mortal Kombat”. As far as Amiga owners were concerned, the title would only be appearing the CD32 and would feature the new Cage ‘Total Immersion Audio’ system providing “stereo sound, serving to ‘punctuate the high-impact game play.’”

The price of Gepro’s “protective spectacles” was dropping to Â£24.99. They purported to completely block “harmful ultraviolet and electromagnetic rays”… hmm…

“All New World of Lemmings” was to be the next instalment in the series, introducing enemies while stripping the mechanics back to “a core of essential abilities for more instinctive playability.”

“Elite” co-creator, Ian Bell, was mulling over whether to take legal action against David Braben’s Frontier Developments for a slice of the “Frontier” pie. Braben felt he had already honoured his commitments.

Speaking of which, “Frontier” publisher GameTek were getting into the Video CD business with the launch of GameTek Cinema. First titles would include cult classics “Toxic Avenger” and “Class of Nuke ‘Em High”.

Trade body ELSPA was to publish a leaflet aimed at parents who were concerned about video games, called “A Parents’ Guide to Computer and Video Games”. It would include a look at the benefits of games, while tackling issues around addiction and violence.

Western Cairo officials shutdown all the arcades citing “moral decay”.

Virgin Megastore launched a radio station to play in all its stores across the country.

A range of coloured mice as on the way from Logitech.

LCL’s “Micro French” and Windshadow Software’ s “Making Music with Bertie Bunny” were two new educational titles.

There was sadly no elaboration as to the reasons why, but the Sega ports of “Rise of the Robots” were “pulled until Easter”, with 3DO and SNES versions also looking likely to join them. Never fear, since the Amiga was still on track to get the game in November.

Features #

  • “Aladdin” Virgin (demo) (A1200 only)
  • “Embryo” Black Legend (demo)

Simon Byron met “Gamesmaster” host Dominic Diamond in a pub for an interview.

Games getting the in-depth Work in Progress treatment this month:

  • “King of Thieves” Team 17 (unreleased)
  • “Premier Manager 3” Realms of Fantasy/Gremlin
  • “Mutant League Hockey” Catfish/Ocean
  • “Powerdrive” Denton Designs/US Gold
  • “Alien Breed: Tower Assault” Team 17

PD games reviewed:

  • “Chilly Chavez” 88%
  • “Formula 1 Challenge” 81%
  • “Introgash” 48%
  • “Tony and Friends in Kelloggs Land” 93%
  • “Nanofly” 50%

Notable Reviews #

  • “Aladdin” Virgin - 90% “It’s safe to say that this film conversion has captured the feel of its movie parent better than any licence before, which is unsurprising when you consider that Disney animators - arguably the best in the world - worked on the original Mega Drive graphics.”
  • “Guardian” Acid Software - 88% “Fortunately, the high difficulty level isn’t too off-putting because the game is such a blimmin’ joy to play. Most of the time everything’s moving along nicely and the game only slows down when the screen’s really, really busy - which isn’t too often.”
  • “Robinson’s Requiem” Silmarils - 81% “Robinson’s Requiem is a game where you have complete freedom over your environment. Every single item you pick up can be used in real life, and all you need to do is figure out which is most logical.”
  • “Zonked!” Data Design/Psygnosis - 85% “Each of the 120 levels is cunningly designed to offer a subtly different challenge to the previous one, so sometimes you’ll find it’s a race against time, whereas others require a conservation of energy.” (unreleased?)
  • “FIFA International Soccer” Visual Sciences/EA - 87% “When you’re playing, a crowd gathers mysteriously around your telly, because you could, quite literally, be watching Match of the Day. On the A500/600 it chugs along a bit, giving the impression of an ‘as a newt’ cameraman as the scrolling struggles to keep up with the play. But it’s very playable - and on an A1200 it’s faster than the console versions.”
