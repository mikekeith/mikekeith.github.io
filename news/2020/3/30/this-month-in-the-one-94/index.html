This month in... The One '94

30 March 2020·5 mins
This Month In... TheOne Amiga Magazines 1994 March

theone199403

News #

This was to be David Upchurch’s last issue as Editor as off to head up EMAP’s relaunch of… Sinclair User. Apparently the Speccy had been seeing a resurgence, though I find that a little hard to believe. At any rate, Simon Byron was to take over the helm.

Team 17 were embracing the CD32 starting with “Ultimate Body Blows”, a revamped version of their beat’em up featuring new AGA backdrops, digitised speech and a digital CD soundtrack. They were also releasing two CD double packs: “Project-X”/“F17 Challenge” and “Alien Breed Special Edition”/“Qwak”. For those poor souls who were still stuck with floppy disks, there was an eight disk compilation comprising of: “Body Blows”, “Superfrog” and “Overdrive”.

Bullfrog signed a new long term contract with Electronic Arts.

Mindscape signed the game rights to Hulk Hogan’s new TV show, “Thunder in Paradise”) (only released on CD-i).

Commodore’s attempts to position the CD32 as more than “just” a games machine received a boost with the news multimedia company Omnimedia was to bring a number of music video titles featuring the likes of David Bowie and Iron Maiden to both the CD32 and Philips’ CD-i player. The FMV cart would be required for the CD32.

The news media had picked up on a story from Which? magazine and the British Heart Foundation which lay the blame for an increase in heart health issues amongst young people in part on video games.

January’s winter Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas saw the US reveal of the CD32. Commodore were bullish about the machine, challenging Sega/Nintendo/Atari/3DO to show “a better games player”. One tidbit that stood out was that Lew Eggebrecht, VP of Engineering, promised 100 titles available at launch this month. The console would retail for $399, with the FMV cart costing an addition $250. Meanwhile, the US would get its first dedicated monthly Amiga mag in “Amiga Game Zone”. Did all this indicate a rejuvination of our beloved machine?

“Battlechess” was making its way to the CD32, though it was unclear whether there would be any enhancements. Interplay were also working on “Dungeon Master II”.

Given the success of the original, it was no surprise to see that Code Masters were working on “Micro Machines 2”. While it wasn’t likely that would see the light of day before then end of the year (or ever on the Amiga), they were planning to release three new budget games on CD (each with “enhanced digital sound”), as well as a CD compilation consisting of five of their best selling titles.

Finnish devs Bloodhouse were working on “Super Stardust” for AGA machines.

Trials of a new interactive TV service had been running in the London region… though it sounded more like a multichoice quiz than anything terribly game-y.

Comic book publisher Dark Horse was launching a new comic called “Max Overload” which would star Lemmings, Chuck Rock and other notable gaming characters.

Hollywood heavy weight MCA was getting into games with the creation of Universal Interactive Studios. Their first title was to be “Jurassic Park Interactive” (only released on the 3DO).

Some kind of industry classification scheme was inevitably going to happen at some point, and ELSPA (European Leisure Software Publisher’s Association) were to begin their voluntary scheme on May 1st. The classification labels would feature a hilariously terrible cartoon character that appeared to be a computer monitor with legs and trainers.

After conquering footie, Sensible Software teased “Sensible Golf”.

Ex-Gamesmaster host Dominic Diamond had a spot on One FM radio every morning at 8:00am where he would present the latest gaming news and reviews. Elsewhere, Bad Influence’s Violet Berlin had joined Digitiser on Channel 4’s Teletext service.

Psygnosis had finally wrapped up “Microcosm” and expected it to be on shelves at time of writing.

Middlesex University were to be the first institution in Europe to offer a game development course in the form of “The Design and Production of Interactive Games.” It was to be run by Domark’s development manager, Russell Ferrier.

Hardware manufacturer Amitek were touting a new external floppy drive for the Amiga which would feature “hardware virus protection”.

Features #

On this month’s two cover disks:

  • “Benefactor” Psygnosis
  • “Ork Attack”
  • “Pollymorf”
  • “Championship Manager Italia” Intelek
  • “Premier Picks”

Part one of a big feature on the Sensible Software lads and their hotly anticipated “Sensible World of Soccer”.

An interview with Intelek who were behind the popular “Championship Manager”.

With the World Cup approaching, The One previewed all the other upcoming Amiga footie games:

  • “World Cup U.S.A. ‘94” US Gold
  • “Kick Off 3” Imagineer/Anco
  • “Sierra World Cup Soccer ‘94” Sierra
  • “Kevin Keegan’s Player Manager” Imagineer/Anco (unreleased under this name?)
  • “On the Ball” Ascon
  • “World Cup Striker” Rage Software (unreleased?)
  • “Brutal Sports Soccer” Millennium (unreleased)
  • “Manchester United: Premier League Champions” Krisalis
  • “FIFA International Soccer” Electronic Arts (there had been no announcement of an Amiga port, but The One were crossing their fingers there would be)

Games getting the indepth Work In Progress this month were:

  • “Guardian” Acid Software
  • “Bump ’n’ Burn” Grandslam
  • “Armourgeddon” Psygnosis
  • “Pinkle” Data Desging/Millennium
  • “Hugo the Troll” Interactive Television Entertainment/Supervision
  • “Turbo Trax” Arcane/Daze

In this month’s PDZone:

  • “Spatial Hyperdrive” Mike J. Rawes 67%
  • “Kung-Fu Charlies” 79%
  • “Nibby Nibble” 90%
  • “Parattack” 81%
  • “Dithell in Space” Darren Ithell 90%

Notable Previews #

  • “UFO: Enemy Unknown” MicroProse
  • “Bubble & Squeak” Audiogenic
  • “Rugby League Coach” Audiogenic
  • “Rabbit Game” Titus

Notable Reviews #

  • “Puggsy” The Dome/Psygnosis - 90% “All credit must go to The Dome for making Puggsy such a technically astounding game - the graphics are of a consistently high standard throughout and the rotating sprites and end-of-level guardians are real eye poppers.”
  • “Championship Manager Italia” Intelek - 89% “It may not appeal to those who prefer flash graphics and gimmicks but in terms of realism and options it can’t be knocked.”
  • “When Two Worlds War” Impressions - 80% “Well, it took me a while to get into it, but I must say that W2WW is a very satisfying strategy romp - it’s just a shame it takes so long to find behind the basic, unappealing presentation and the primitive, less-than-friendly control system.”

Charts #

  2. “Frontier: Elite 2” Frontier/Gametek
  3. “The Settlers” Blue Byte
