This month in... The One '95

Posted on 30 April 2021 18:52 NZST by Michael Keith

Category: This month in...

News

The One had had a bit of a facelift with the Contents page getting a re- design, along with various sections getting re-labelled.

The big news for the month was that Commodore UK had announced a new Amiga: the A1(M). The specs included “32 megabytes of RAM, a 1 gigabyte hard drive, 32-bit graphics card, 64-bit processor, 8x speed CD-ROM drive”. And of course it was The One’s April Fools.

Thalion had hit on their hands with “A320 Airbus” which had shifted over 250,000 copies, so it was no surprise they were working on the follow-up, “Approach Trainer”.

The highly anticipated (perhaps a slight exaggeration) “Street Fighter” movie had arrived and The One’s critical verdict? “Poo”.

“Kung Fu bunnies, kick-boxing foxes, and wrestling bears” featured in Gametek’s new beat-’em-up, “Brutal: Paws of Fury”.

Some bloke named Steve Minton had started a new user group for fans for “Frontier”. Interested people were to mail him their save games and some “Frontier” tips.

I guess getting a mention as a sign there was still a little life in our favourite machine, retailer Silica had put out its new Amiga catalogue.

“Gloom” looked like it might provide a decent Doom-ish experience.

Impressions reckoned their “Ultimate Soccer Manager” would be “the last word in football strategy games”.

While Microprose hadn’t been keen on the idea of bringing any new titles to the Amiga, they were porting “Colonization”.

Gametek had acquired the licence to “The X-Files”.

The One acknowledged the passing of Lt Commander Sir Marshall George Clitheroe Warmington, 3rd Bart, aged 84. Why? Because he was apparently the first person in the 20th century to be called “Pants”….

The National Museum of Computer and Video games opened… in St Louis… in the US…

Probe Software changed its name to Probe Entertainment.

A drop in general retail sales during January were blamed on the National Lottery.

Warner Interactive Entertainment followed its acquisition of Renegade by signing Sensible Software for their next three titles and an option on every following game. They had been up against Virgin, which would still publish “Sensible Golf”.

Features

On this month’s two cover disks:

  • “X-Fighter” Thalion (demo) (full game was never released)
  • “Flight of the Amazon Queen” Renegade

Sensible Software’s Jon Hare popped up in the Letters section to answer a slew of “Sensible World of Soccer” related questions & gripes from readers.

A light hearted ratings roundup of beat’em up fighter fashions.

Microprose PR Manager Jason Dutton was subjected to the “Eliza Files” interview (first half questions asked by a journo, second half him conversing with software therapist Eliza).

Getting the in-depth Work In Progress treatment this month:

  • “Super Street Fighter 2” Freestyle Games/US Gold
  • “Lost Eden” Cryo (unreleased)
  • “Player Manager 2” Steve Screech/Anco
  • “Chaos Engine 2” Bitmap Brothers/Renegade (Part 4.. the penultimate?)
  • “Baldies” Creative Edge/Gametek (unreleased)
  • “The Speris Legacy” Binary Emotions/Team 17
  • “Final Over” Team 17 (unreleased)

PD Games covered:

  • “Zaxxon” 78%
  • “Bubble Trubble” 89%
  • “Aquanaut” 93%
  • “Charr” 77%
  • “Graphic Adventure Creator (inc. Lethal Formula)” 88%

Notable Reviews

  • “Skidmarks 2” Acid Software - 88% “…. apart from some neat additions tacked on to the main game, this could actually be Skidmarks. Where it does score brownie points, though, is with the sheer madness of it all.”
  • “Dawn Patrol” Rowan/Empire - 81% “Still, the fact is that Rowan’s engine is a good one, and on balance, and after much split-arseing about with my conscience, I reckon Dawn Patrol is Rowan’s best yet, especially if you don’t own either of the earlier games.”
  • “Ambermoon” Thalion - 84% “Great areas of the screen judder past as you struggle to shift about from one room to the other, spoiling what should be an exciting and spooky aspect of Ambermoon. Once you get used to the poor scrolling, though, the underlying game appears and reassures you that your money wasn’t wasted.”

Charts

  • “Sensible World of Soccer” Sensible Software/Renegade
  • “Pinball Illusions” Digital Illusions/21st Century
  • “FIFA Soccer” EA
