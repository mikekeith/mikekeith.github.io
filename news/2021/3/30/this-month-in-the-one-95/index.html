This month in... The One '95

Posted on 30 March 2021 19:43 NZDT by Michael Keith

Category: This month in...

News

Despite some recent colossal flops at the box office (“Mario Brothers” and “Double Dragon”) it seemed the success of “Street Fighter” was enough for Hollywood to take another stab it with “Mortal Kombat”. The only “big names” attached to it were Christopher Lambert and Talisa Soto (a Bond girl in “Licence to Kill”). Unknown Paul Anderson was to direct. Elsewhere there were rumours of movies based on “Doom” and “Myst”.

Ocean’s budget label Hit Squad was adding a new title each month through June: “Jurassic Park”, “Space Hulk”, “Cannon Fodder” and “Syndicate”.

Thalion’s “Ambermoon” for the CD32 was finally making its way to the UK after initially only being released in continental Europe.

A rather surprising book tie-in was being written. Jim Murdoch’s “Rise of the Robots” would be published by Penguin.

There was a compatibility list for CD32 games running on the Zappo A1200 CD drive.

The One received an unusual demand from ICE Software regarding their upcoming “Akira” game. They were not releasing review copies to journos “until our forthcoming trip to the USA”. However, they had already supplied The One with a copy and stipulated that a review could only be printed if the score was higher than 80%. There was no review in this issue.

Gremlin invested in a motion capture system from Motion Analysis. Meanwhile they were to release an add on for “Premier Manager 3”. The “Premier Manager Multi-Edit System” would allow players to make their own updates to the league.

Canada’s DEM Software were having a crack at a “Doom” clone with “Angst”.

It’d been nearly a year since stocks of new Amigas ran out and The One summarised what kind of prices you could expect if you wanted to pick up a used one.

Grandslam were working on a new platformer, “Ruffian”.

The One, in a clear state of frustration, refused to even attempt to try to write up “news” regarding the fate of Commodore.

Electronic Arts acquired Bullfrog for a cool $45 million. The US game giant insisted Bullfrog would maintain complete control and even be able to license some titles to other publishers.

Ultramind’s “RelaxPlus” claimed to enable thought control of games via a bio- sensor which “attaches to a player’s skin”.

TV Gladiator Shadow’s alleged “alarming” cocaine habit forced Gremlin to scramble to replace him with Hunter in their ad campaign for “Shadow Fighter”.

Team 17 were apparently working on a cricket game which looked somewhat like Sensible’s recent titles (unreleased).

Features

On this month’s two cover disks:

  • “Valhalla: Before the War” Vulcan (demo)
  • “FIFA Soccer” Electronic Arts (demo)

An interview with Kiwi game devs Acid Software.

Despite industry body ELPSA’s introduction of a voluntary ratings system for games, the UK government’s new legislation meant games would now be subject to the same way of classification as videos. The One investigated what this would mean for gamers.

Games getting in-depth Work in Progress articles this month:

  • “Worms” Team 17
  • “Alien Breed 3D” Team 17
  • “Chaos Engine 2” The Bitmap Brothers/Renegade (Part 3…)
  • “Pussies Galore” Amber Developments/Team 17 (unreleased)
  • “Frontier 2: First Encounters” Frontier Developments/Gametek (unreleased)

PD games reviewed:

  • “Blast ’em!” 87%
  • “Island” POO (yes, that was the rating)
  • “Captain Carnage” 51%
  • “Punter v2.2” 79%
  • “Carwars” 30%

Notable Reviews

  • “ATR” Team 17 - 85% “When you first buy ATR, you’ll find it annoying, but when you get into the game, it just gets better and better. Highly polished, well-presented, nicely-executed. Great.”
  • “Roketz” The Farm/Exclusive PD (A1200) - 85% “You might need a few goes against the computer in order to get the hang of things but, my dears, it’s got to be said, Roketz is a lovely little two-player game.”
  • “Speedball 2” The Bitmap Brothers/Renegade (CD32) - 83% “If I was looking at this as an Update, there wouldn’t really be too much to comment on, but the new music is superb, and the inclusion of more commentator samples and crowd effects boosts the atmosphere considerably.”
  • “The Complete Chess System” Oxford Soft - 80% “If you don’t have an interest in chess but think you might like to have a bash one day, this is the sort of package that will be a valuable from beginner level right up to when you consider yourself a real chess old master.”

Charts

  1. “Sensible World of Soccer” Sensible Software/Renegade
  2. “FIFA Soccer” EA
  3. “Theme Park” EA
